# Taskflow PRD (v3.0)

_One hub. One AI. Every part of your life — finally connected._

---

## 1) One-Liner

Taskflow is an AI-powered personal life operating system built around a hub-and-satellite model — a central hub that connects your tasks, habits, accountability, and calendar with a growing ecosystem of standalone apps covering fitness, nutrition, journaling, mindfulness, finance, and goals. The more of your life you bring in, the smarter the AI gets, and the harder it becomes to leave.

---

## 2) The Real Problem

Modern life is fragmented. People who want to take control of their health, productivity, and finances are forced to spread themselves across 6, 8, sometimes 10 different apps — none of which talk to each other.

- A task app that doesn't know you skipped the gym
- A habit tracker that doesn't know you're over budget
- A fitness app that doesn't know you haven't slept well
- A meal planner that doesn't know your schedule
- A journal that connects to nothing
- A meditation app that never checks if you're actually less stressed
- A goal tracker with no idea whether you're moving toward your goals or just documenting them
- Generic AI tools with zero context about who you actually are

The result: data everywhere, insight nowhere. People are busy tracking their lives without actually improving them.

**No platform currently connects the dots across all of these domains and uses that full picture to actively coach someone toward a better life.**

---

## 3) The Solution

Taskflow is the hub that ties everything together.

At its core, Taskflow handles the daily foundation:

- **Tasks** — daily planning, priorities, to-dos
- **Habits** — build and track positive routines
- **Accountability** — custom streak trackers, resets, and reflection
- **Notes & Journal** — capture thoughts, reflect, and spot patterns
- **Calendar** — plan your week and month

Over time, Taskflow expands through a **satellite app ecosystem** — seven standalone apps that each solve a specific problem on their own, but feed their data directly into Taskflow's AI brain. Each satellite is a fully functional product. Each one is also a door into the ecosystem.

Taskflow's AI sits at the center of all of it. It doesn't just read your tasks — it reads your whole life, connects what no single app can, and tells you what's actually going on:

> _"Your task completion drops every week you miss workouts. You've missed three in a row."_
> _"You tend to overspend when you haven't journaled in a few days."_
> _"Your most productive days start with a logged meal and a completed morning habit."_
> _"Your stress levels in journal entries spike on weeks you skip your meditation streak."_
> _"You've hit your goal check-ins every day this week — your habit consistency is at an all-time high."_

That cross-domain intelligence is what no single-category app can offer. That's the Taskflow difference.

### The Flywheel

Each satellite earns its own paying users through its specific niche. Those users discover Taskflow Hub through in-app prompts. Once connected, the AI starts correlating across domains — and that moment of insight is the reason users never leave. The more connected apps a user has, the higher their lifetime value and the lower their churn. Users with 3+ apps connected churn at a fraction of the rate of single-app users.

### The Integration Moment

When a satellite app user connects to Taskflow, they unlock the insight layer — the AI can now correlate their fitness data with their productivity, their nutrition with their mood, their finances with their journaling tone. That moment is the product's most powerful experience and the primary reason users stay. It is engineered, not accidental.

### Model Overview

Each app — the hub and every satellite — has its own **free tier and paid subscription**. Users pay only for what they use. The bundle exists for users who want everything at a significant discount.

### Individual App Pricing

| App              | Free               | Plus  | Pro   |
| ---------------- | ------------------ | ----- | ----- |
| Taskflow Hub     | Core features      | $5/mo | $9/mo |
| Taskflow Lift    | Basic logging      | $4/mo | $7/mo |
| Taskflow Eats    | Basic planning     | $4/mo | $7/mo |
| Taskflow Journal | Basic journaling   | $3/mo | $5/mo |
| Taskflow Calm    | Basic sessions     | $4/mo | $7/mo |
| Taskflow Finance | Basic tracking     | $4/mo | $8/mo |
| Taskflow Goals   | Basic goal setting | $3/mo | $5/mo |

**Individual Pro total (all 7 apps): ~$48/mo**

### The Bundle

**Taskflow All-Access — $20/mo (monthly) / $16/mo (billed yearly)**

Everything. All 7 apps. All Pro features. One price. Saves the user over 55% compared to subscribing individually.

The bundle is the primary conversion target. It has higher LTV, significantly lower churn, and creates switching costs that make users extremely sticky. A user with 3+ apps connected to the hub does not leave.

### Checkout Upsell Strategy

When any user navigates to the pricing page of any satellite app and is about to subscribe, they are shown a **bundle intercept banner before the individual plans**. The banner:

- Appears above the app's individual pricing tiers
- Shows the full list of all 7 apps with the combined individual cost ($48/mo)
- Offers the bundle at $20/mo with the savings prominently displayed
- Has a primary CTA: "Get the bundle" and a secondary dismiss: "No thanks, just [App Name]"
- Does not hide the individual plans — they remain visible but visually secondary

The timing is deliberate. The user already has their wallet out. They're in "pay" mode. Presenting the bundle at that moment — before they commit to just one app — is the highest-conversion point in the entire funnel.

### Revenue Drivers

- **Single-app users**: $4–9/mo per user, moderate churn
- **Hub + 1 satellite**: ~$12–16/mo, lower churn
- **Hub + 3 satellites**: ~$20–30/mo, very low churn
- **Bundle subscribers**: $20/mo flat, minimal churn, highest LTV
- **Target**: Drive users toward the bundle through discovery, not pressure

## 14) Retention Philosophy — The Premium Drop

### The Core Principle

Taskflow does not make the free tier bad. It makes the premium tier feel like a different reality — and going back feels like loss.

This is the same mechanism Spotify engineered deliberately. Free Spotify is functional. But premium users who downgrade don't just lose features — they feel the silence. Ads interrupt the flow. Shuffle takes control away. The audio quality drops. The experience that felt effortless now feels like friction at every turn. That contrast — that drop — is worth more than any marketing campaign.

Taskflow operates on the same principle, at a scale Spotify can't touch. Because Taskflow isn't just music. It's the user's entire life.

---

### How the Drop Is Built

**Step 1 — The free tier earns real investment.**

Users don't just sign up and browse. They build. They log habits and watch streaks grow. They write journal entries. They complete tasks and see their patterns take shape. They connect a satellite app and start feeding in real data. By week three, the free tier isn't just an app — it's a record of who they've been trying to become. That data is theirs. That investment is emotional, not transactional.

**Step 2 — The AI shows them the edge of the curtain.**

Free users get glimpses. The AI surfaces one insight — _"you've been more consistent this week"_ — but the full pattern analysis is locked behind Pro. They see that something deeper exists. They got a taste. Now they want the whole picture. The upgrade is no longer a feature decision. It's curiosity.

**Step 3 — Premium feels like the app finally woke up.**

The moment a user upgrades, everything sharpens. The AI knows them at a deeper level. The daily plan feels personal instead of generic. Cross-app insights start firing. The AI isn't just reading tasks anymore — it's reading their workouts, their spending, their journal tone, their goal momentum, and connecting things the user never connected themselves. It doesn't feel like unlocking features. It feels like Taskflow finally became what it was always supposed to be.

**Step 4 — The drop.**

If a user ever downgrades, the silence is immediate. The insights vanish. The daily plan feels hollow. The AI that knew them — really knew them — is gone. The app still works. But it no longer feels like theirs. That drop is not a bug. It is a feature. It is the most powerful retention mechanism in the product.

---

### Why the Ecosystem Makes the Drop Exponential

A Spotify premium user who downgrades loses audio quality and shuffle control. That hurts.

A Taskflow premium user who downgrades loses the AI that has been reading their workout frequency, their emotional tone across hundreds of journal entries, their spending patterns, their nutrition consistency, their goal momentum — every signal they've fed into the ecosystem for months.

The more satellite apps a user connects, the more data lives inside Taskflow's AI brain. And the more data lives there, the more catastrophic the drop becomes. One connected app creates investment. Three connected apps creates dependency. Five connected apps creates something that feels irreplaceable.

**The more of your life you put in, the more painful it is to leave.**

That is not a subscription model. That is gravity.

---

### Free Tier Design Rules (Non-Negotiable)

These rules exist to protect the drop and ensure the free tier builds investment rather than frustration:

- **Free must feel genuinely useful.** A user who feels cheated on the free tier never upgrades — they leave. The free tier earns trust.
- **Free must create real data.** Streaks, habits, journal entries, logged workouts — all of this is the user's investment. The more real data they create, the stronger the upgrade pull.
- **The AI on free shows glimpses, never nothing.** One insight is enough to create desire. Zero insights creates indifference.
- **Premium never feels like a paywall.** It feels like a reveal. The user isn't being blocked — they're being shown what's possible.
- **The drop must be felt immediately on downgrade.** Not gradually. The contrast should be unmistakable within the first session back on free.
