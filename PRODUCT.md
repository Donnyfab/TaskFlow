# Product

## Register

product

## Users

TaskFlow serves intentional adults who already track parts of their lives, such as training, habits, budgets, notes, or tasks, and want one serious operating surface instead of scattered tools. They are not first-time productivity users. They open the app to orient the day, stay honest about commitments, and see how effort connects across routines.

## Product Purpose

TaskFlow is a personal life OS for planning, task tracking, habit work, notes, journaling, focus, and AI-assisted reflection. Its purpose is to give users clarity and momentum through a command-center experience: fast orientation, accountable priorities, and useful synthesis without cheerleading or gamification.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Accountable. Uncluttered.

The interface should feel serious, restrained, and respectful of the user's time. Copy should be direct and useful, closer to a sharp editor's toolkit than a productivity coach. The emotional goal is clarity and momentum.

## Anti-references

TaskFlow should not look or feel like Notion's soft minimalism, Todoist's chirpy productivity, pastel gamification, startup pink-and-blue gradients, gradient text, glowing cards, confetti-like streaks, or bouncy encouragement loops.

Reference qualities to preserve: Arc's focused premium feel, Linear's velocity and surgical spacing, and early Superhuman's seriousness about every pixel.

## Design Principles

1. Command center, not cheerleader. Every surface should give the user control and orientation, never encouragement theater.
2. Restraint as signal. The dark monochromatic palette communicates seriousness; accent color appears only when something meaningful occurred.
3. Hierarchy through precision, not decoration. Weight, size, spacing, and opacity carry the interface. Avoid cards-on-cards, gradient text, and decorative borders.
4. AI has a voice, not a costume. AI-generated content needs subtle visual distinction through weight, texture, or restrained amber treatment, not a separate product language.
5. One feature at a time feels complete. Each page should feel resolved rather than half-finished or visually bolted on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA unless a future feature requires a stricter bar. Preserve reduced-motion alternatives; motion should orient state changes in roughly 80-120 ms and should never bounce or linger. Do not rely on color alone for state or AI-origin signals; pair the amber accent with structure, labels, weight, or texture.
